# Game Mechanics

## Overview

The British Military Life Simulator is a career progression game where players create and manage a military career through the British Armed Forces.

## Character Creation

### Entry Points

1. **Junior Entry (Age 16)**
   - AFC Harrogate (British Army)
   - RAF Halton (Royal Air Force)
   - HMS Raleigh (Royal Navy)
   - CTC Lympstone (Royal Marines)

2. **Direct Entry (Age 18+)**
   - Enlisted rank positions
   - AOSB for officer aspirants

3. **Officer Commission**
   - AOSB (Officer Selection)
   - Sandhurst (Training)
   - Regimental Selection Board

### Career Attributes

- **Service**: Army, Navy, RAF, Marines, UKSF
- **Rank**: Enlisted through to Field Marshal
- **Specialisation**: Role-specific skills
- **Experience**: Years of service
- **Skills**: Combat, Leadership, Technical, etc.
- **Fitness**: Physical capability
- **Mental Health**: Stress and morale management

## Career Progression

### Rank Advancement

- Enlisted → NCO → Warrant Officer → Officer (for selected few)
- Promotion courses required for advancement
- Performance evaluations affect promotion speed

### Specialisations

- Role-specific training (Infantry, Pilot, Engineer, etc.)
- Advanced courses (JTAC, Diver, EOD, P Coy, etc.)
- Display team selection after sufficient experience

### Deployments

- Rotational deployments to global locations
- Combat operations in designated conflict zones
- Training exercises
- Humanitarian missions

## Personal Life

### Relationships & Family

- Build relationships with colleagues and civilians
- Marriage/Partnership possibilities
- Children with associated costs and time management
- Family events affecting morale

### Lifestyle

- Hobbies and interests
- Health management
- Financial planning
- Discipline and conduct system

## Gameplay Systems

### Time Management

- Game time progresses in months
- Each action costs time
- Career events occur based on time progression

### Events

- Promotions
- Course completions
- Deployments
- Personal milestones
- Disciplinary actions
- Medals/Decorations

### Interaction Options

- Choose career paths
- Manage personal relationships
- Participate in training
- Accept/Decline assignments
- Plan career development

## Victory/Conclusion Conditions

- Career to age 65 (Full Service)
- Early discharge (medical or voluntary)
- Post-service career establishment
- Reservist continuation
- Achievement milestones
